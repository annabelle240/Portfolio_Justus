About Me

Hi! I am years old in my third year at RWTH Aachen working towards my BSc. in Computer Science. Beyond that, I am passionate about Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing research, where I am particularly interested in security topics such as privacy and robustness to both adversarial inputs and natural distribution shifts.

I am currently affiliated with the Empirical Inference Department at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, where I am doing research under the supervision of Zhijing Jin.

I also frequently work together with the Language, Reasoning and Education Lab at ETH Zürich.

Previously, I’ve spent time at SAP Security Research where I worked on Differentially Private Data Generation under the supervision of Benjamin Weggenmann and at RWTH Aachen, where I conducted research on psycholinguistically motivated NLP together with Elma Kerz and Daniel Wiechmann.

I’ve also had a great time interning at DynamoFL (YC W22).

I am currently living in Aachen and occasionally spend time in Frankfurt am Main, where my family lives. In my freetime, I like to practice Luta Livre.
